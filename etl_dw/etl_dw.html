A simple and deep dive into ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) and Data Warehousing. Learn step by step with visuals, examples, and a fun quiz.

What is ETL?

ETL stands for Extract, Transform, Load. Think of it like making fruit juice ü•§:

  • Extract: Pick fruits from the farm (get raw data from sources).
  • Transform: Wash, cut, and blend them (clean and format the data).
  • Load: Pour into a glass (store in a database/warehouse).

Advanced Concepts Made Easy

  • Staging Area: A temporary kitchen where raw data is kept before cooking.
  • Star Schema: A design where one big table (fact) connects to smaller helper tables (dimensions).
  • OLAP: Online Analytical Processing ‚Äì fast querying for analysis.
  • ELT vs ETL: In cloud, data is often loaded first (ELT) and transformed later.
